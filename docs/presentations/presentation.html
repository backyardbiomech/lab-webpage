Transfer and substitution rules

Problem: Especially with the newer soon-to-publish transfer portal, it will appear to interested students that our major will not accept their transfer credits, and therefore require more credits than other institutions, even though it may not.

Transfer and substitution rules

flowchart LR
  A[ BIO 101 ] --> B( BIOL 101 ) 
  C[ BIOL 141 ] --> D( BIOL 206 or 2XX elective)
  E[ BIOL 142 ] --> F( BIOL 207 or 2XX elective)
  G[ BIOL 150 Micro HltSci ] --> H( BIOL 1XX elec. or BIOL 304 Micro HltSci)
  I[ BIOL 205 Gen. Micro ] --> J( BIOL 304 Micro HltSci)

these are all “non majors” courses

Transfer and substitution rules

Since BIOL 101, 206, 207, 304 don’t apply to the major, the transfer portal makes it appear that students with these classes need to take more credits.

E.g. A student with BIO 101, 141, 142, and 205 at a CC. They enter that into the portal which tells they need

  • 49 core biology credits in BIES for bio major
  • ca. 41 for KINS
  • ca. 31 for JMU biology!

But

We generally allow substituting for → 120, 301, 302, 305 when major is declared

flowchart LR
  A[ BIO 101 ] --> B( BIOL 101 ) -.-> K( BIOL 120)
  C[ BIOL 141 ] --> D( BIOL 206 or 2XX elective) -.-> L( BIOL 301 )
  E[ BIOL 142 ] --> F( BIOL 207 or 2XX elective) -.-> M( BIOL 302 )
  G[ BIOL 150 HltSci ] --> H( BIOL 1XX elec. or BIOL 304 Micro HltSci) -.-> N( BIOL 305 Gen. Micro)
  I[ BIOL 205 Gen. Micro ] --> J( BIOL 304 Micro HltSci) -.-> N

Proposals

  • We add a note like “This course may substitute as BIOL 120 for Biology Majors” to the Course Notes section of the transfer portal. But this doesn’t fix the credit counting.

and/or

  • We add 120/301/302 as transfer options (the new portal will allow multiple options)
    • not only good for attracting students, but should fix registration errors
